# Launch runbook

The whole launch, in posting order, executable by hand in about an hour of active
work on day one plus a few five-minute follow-ups across the week. Nothing here
auto-posts; every step is Jovan pasting final text into a platform. Final text for
every post lives in this directory.

## Before anything (ten minutes)

1. Open https://github.com/The-825/agent-ops-playbook in a logged-out window. The
   README is the landing page every post funnels to: confirm it renders, the pitch
   reads right, and the latest CI run on main is green. Fix the front door before
   inviting traffic.
2. Confirm the one-time Substack setup is done (the checklist in
   substack-series.md).
3. Pick the day: Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday, starting between 8 and 10am
   Pacific.
4. Skim each target sub's sidebar rules one last time. The posting notes in
   reddit-posts.md were verified 2026-07-21; sidebars shift.

## Day one, the launch hour

| When | Channel | Source | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| T+0 | Substack post 1 | substack-series.md | Publish, then restack to Notes with one line in your own words |
| T+15 | LinkedIn post 1 | linkedin-posts.md | Body link stays unless reach dies; then move it to a first comment |
| T+30 | r/ClaudeAI | reddit-posts.md post 1 | Flair Built with Claude; needs an account over 50 karma, else use the showcase megathread |

Substack goes first because it is the durable home of the story and the only step
with setup weight; everything after it is paste and go. Stop after three channels on
day one. Three comment threads is one person's load.

## The rest of the week

| Day | Channel | Source | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 2, morning | Show HN, optional | show-hn.md | Only on a morning you can watch the thread; retype the comment in your own words |
| Day 3 | r/AI_Agents | reddit-posts.md post 2 | Text post, no body link; the repo goes in your first comment. Day 2 if you skip HN |
| Day 4 or 5 | r/dataengineering | reddit-posts.md post 3 | The strictest sub and your one self-promo slot there for the month; disclosure comment immediately after posting |
| Day 8 | LinkedIn post 2 | linkedin-posts.md | Same mid-week morning window |
| Day 15 | LinkedIn post 3 | linkedin-posts.md | Same window |

Why the spacing: the same repo link landing across five communities in one morning
reads as a campaign. The same link shared by one person over a week reads as a
person sharing their work, and it keeps every comment thread answerable.

## What to watch, per channel

- GitHub: stars and issues. A drive-by issue from a stranger is the funnel working;
  answer it the same day.
- Substack: restacks and follows on Notes. There is no mailing list yet, so ignore
  email stats.
- LinkedIn: reach in the first two hours. If the body link visibly hurts, edit it
  out and add it as your first comment.
- Reddit: removals and modmail first, then comment velocity. If a post is removed,
  message the mods, ask which rule and where the right home is, and follow their
  answer. Never repost without one.
- Show HN: the thread itself. Answer every technical question in the first two
  hours; slow starts are the median outcome.

## Answering comments in your voice

Same rules as everything in this directory: plain English, answer first, concrete
files and incidents over adjectives, no em-dashes, nothing that sounds generated.

- The skeptic ("overkill for one person"): agree with the premise, correct the
  frame. Most PRs are opened by agents, so the discipline is for the staff, not the
  headcount. Every rule maps to an incident.
- "Show numbers": give the honest scale markers (since 2025, several sessions a day,
  a production system with regulated PII, one human) and say plainly that you do
  not publish invented metrics.
- "AI wrote this": yes, agents helped build the repo; that is what the repo is
  about. The posts and replies are yours. Say it once, calmly, and move on.
- The technical challenge (usually the automerge doc): the best possible thread.
  Engage fully, concede what is fair in one sentence, then add the detail behind
  the rest.
- "Where is the book": you are finishing it; the repo README carries the link the
  day it is live. Nothing more. The repo sells the practice, the book sells the
  story (ruling D-4).
- Pricing, dates, launch logistics: not in any reply, per the standing rules in
  marketing/README.md.

## If something goes sideways

- A Reddit removal is feedback, not a crisis. Ask the mods, adjust, move on.
- An HN submission cannot be deleted once it has replies. If the thread goes badly,
  answer what is answerable and let it sink; never delete and repost the same day.
- A factual error in a post: edit where the platform allows (Reddit, LinkedIn,
  Substack), reply with the correction where it does not (HN). One line, no
  apology spiral.
