From Archivist to Architect

A Data Analyst's Field Guide to Becoming a Data Architect

Jovan Smith

Buy on Amazon Get the free companion repo

Published by 825 Consulting Press, the publishing imprint of 825 Consulting LLC.

Most academic and nonprofit offices run on data debt: a spreadsheet nobody fully trusts and one person nobody can afford to lose.

If you have ever spent a Friday afternoon copy-pasting student records, reconciling forms that should not need reconciling, or trying to remember which version of "the file" is current, this book is for you.

From Archivist to Architect is a practical framework for modernizing the data infrastructure of any helper-staffed office: academic advising programs, TRiO and other federally-funded support services, K-12 outreach, small nonprofits, civic technology teams. Written by a higher-education data architect who built and deployed this architecture inside a top-ranked Honors program at a major California public university, the book hands you the operating model, not just the tools.

You will learn

  • How to audit the hidden cost of your current spreadsheet-and-IT-tickets workflow (the "Data Debt" calculator alone justifies the book)
  • How to ask your IT department for the right thing in the right way (the IT Translator email template, ready to paste)
  • How to build a Level 3 data architecture using BigQuery, Google Sheets, and AppScript: tools your team already has access to
  • How to turn institutional policy into automated, auditable logic without losing the human judgment that makes your office work
  • How to defend the rebuild to your dean, director, or executive sponsor with numbers that hold up
  • How to scale the same architecture across multiple programs without doubling your team

Built on global standards. No vendor lock-in. No engineering degree required.

If you have inherited a fragile system, if you are about to be hired into one, or if you are quietly planning the migration off your office's brittle database or sprawling tracker spreadsheet, start here.
